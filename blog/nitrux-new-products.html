Blog: Nitrux new products

Posted on January 21, 2014 by Uri Herrera

In the coming months we'll be preparing to include two new products to our blooming hardware product line or rather two new product ranges, one will be a new PC product line featuring a rather affordable but high-end Steam Machine and a second PC intended for small businesses with the option to choose from 5 distributions. Besides of course our offering of the NXQ® lodead with Nitrux® OS, together with this we will redesign the Nitrux Store® to fit all of this and serve you better.

In terms of specifications our Steam machine will have the following:

  • • Intel Core i7 4770K (water-cooled)
  • • 8 or 16 GB DDR3 RAM
  • • Kingston 240 GB SSD (RAID0 - 2x 120 GB)
  • • Radeon HD 270X 2 GB GDDR5
  • • Custom Mini ITX case

We'll try to make it as affordable as possible even considering it's a high-end gaming rig. Of course it will be powered by the latest available Steam OS from Valve. We're aiming to release it a couple of months after the NXQ® starts shipping. The second PC it's a more scaled down version of it, not primarily intended for gaming of course, but for those small businesses that require a solid platform to rely on, from this you'll be available to choose from these distributions: Fedora, openSUSE, Debian, RedHat and Arch Linux.

In terms of specifications our second PC will have the following:

  • • Intel Core i3 4130
  • • 8 or 16 GB DDR3 RAM
  • • Kingston 120 GB SSD
  • • Custom Mini ITX case

It will come preloaded with any of those distributions but with a twist, you will be able to select what software it comes lodaded with (or extra tweaks like theming) so you get the PC just like you want it to be this at no extra cost of course or if you want it with only a minimal instllation (netinstall in the case of Debian or using the core packages in Arch Linux, etc). On a final note, just as with the NXQ®, every 4,000 USD we make out of the sales of these will be given to any Open Source® project through Backd (yes, yes, we're still working on it). As for the Bitcoin miner we mentioned at some point, we're probably going to give that a second thought and see how Bitcoin or other altcoins develop in the future.
