Blog: Nitrux site revamp and news

Posted on June 09, 2014 by Uri Herrera

How time flies huh?, it's been quite a long time since a post was made and with good reason, we have been working on stuff like Typer.IM our new private messaging service made with node.js, we have also been making plans for our next projects to come to fruition, we have been helping KDE with their new icon theme, and most visibly we have been working on a new look for the site. Needless to say that means that we didn't updated our icon themes for what would be now a month (we know it's been along time without updates) now that we have finished the site revamp we can go back to our other stuff. The site looked good, but good is never enough so we made some changes, quite a lot actually.

There's no more tabs except in the Nitrux Store but we'll get to that, no tabs means less hassle on our end. The site now pays attention to a more visual experience. We kept the same layout that we were using so that it remains familiar to you, but we felt that it lacked more visual appealing thus we made extensive use of images. We've also used an even more modern look for things like the buttons or the forms. You may notice text is a different color too and so is the font. We're now using Roboto instead of Source Sans as the font for the entire site. We've also used a look inspired by the Xperience UI concepts in the form of blurred backgrounds with either dark or clear overlays.

The images from the headers are also different, now they are entirely custom made for that specific purpose. We also started to use the Font Awesome icon font (a freely available glyph font) to identify the categories within what is now a new form of menu of the site's content just below the header in a similar fashion to various desktop environments and OSes settings.

On the side of the site's content, the Home page retains the fade-in animation it had but now it's not that slow to load, the Developer site now features clearer content and makes use of the newly improved forms, The Nitrux Store still has the previous look and that is because we're intending to add features of a proper e-commerce site, specially the shopping cart or dropdown menus for shipping selection, etc. so it will look the same until we figure out a way to add those features, checkout will be processed by PayPal as it currently is so we won't require you to make an account or anything similar to buy stuff at our store.

The Software and Hardware sections heavily feature images now and definitely in a more appealing way than before. The Company section has an added bonus: you can download our brand assets so you can feature the correct logos when talking about us (wink), inside you'll find logos and a style guide. Along with it the section that features us now has links to our social network sites if you want to add us and of course a nice animation for our partner logos.

Now the news, first off we're introducing 3 new lines of products and 1 that we're doing along Numix Project :

  • NXQ - Continuation of the QtBox and our new mini-PC range.
  • NXP - Our Android smart-phone range.
  • NXT - Our Android tablet range.
  • Ozon Steambox - Gaming PC range in collaboration with Numix project.

We decided against using silly names for our product ranges like SuperPhone Andromeda X1 S5 compact 5" SPHM-658 and went with simple names as above. The names explained:

  • NXQ: NX being Nitrux (or just look at our logo) and the Q in honor of the QtBox.
  • NXP: Same as above. The P stands for phone.
  • NXT: Same as the others. The T stands for tablet.

So having explained that let's get into some information about them. First the phone and tablet won't be available until next year. The Ozon Steambox is a work in progress and you should see it coming out soon and finally the NXQ range will be available starting today.

With that said don't forget we appreciate your support when you buy stuff from the Nitrux Store and remember that you hold a special place in our hearts for doing so.
